# Detail Controls

Use this reference when the user asks for a specific length, "精简", "详细", "deep", "brief", "short", "long", "report", or when choosing a reasonable output size.

## Priority Order

1. User's explicit word, character, page, or section count.
2. User's requested format, such as quick scan, brief, battlecard, memo, or deep report.
3. Decision risk: higher-stakes decisions need more evidence and caveats.
4. Default mode from `SKILL.md`.

If the requested length conflicts with the requested scope, say what will be compressed or offer to split into parts.

## Length Modes

| Mode | Chinese Target | English Target | Competitors | Profile Depth | Best For |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- | --- |
| Ultra brief | 300-600 chars | 180-350 words | 3 | None or 1-line | quick gut check |
| Quick scan | 800-1,400 chars | 500-900 words | 3-5 | 1-2 bullets each | early idea screen |
| Standard brief | 1,800-3,500 chars | 1,200-2,200 words | 5-8 | compact profile | product positioning |
| Detailed report | 4,500-9,000 chars | 3,000-6,000 words | 8-12 | full profile | launch/investment/pricing |
| Staged diligence | multiple parts | multiple parts | 10-15+ | full profile + evidence register | high-stakes deep research |

Targets are approximate and usually exclude tables, citations, and source lists.

## Detail Levels

### Level 1: Executive

Use when the user wants a fast answer.

- One-line market definition.
- Compact competitor table.
- 3 threats, 3 opportunities.
- Clear recommendation.
- No long evidence register.

### Level 2: Operator

Default for strategy work.

- Competitor matrix.
- Compact profiles for important competitors.
- Opportunity map.
- Pricing/GTM implications when relevant.
- 90-day moves and watchlist.

### Level 3: Diligence

Use for serious product, investment, launch, pricing, or roadmap decisions.

- Segmented landscape.
- Full competitor profiles.
- Evidence labels and source-quality notes.
- Scored matrix with rationale.
- Risks, unknowns, assumptions, and validation plan.

## Compression Rules

When the requested length is short:

- Keep executive take, matrix, opportunities, and recommendation.
- Cut background market explanation first.
- Replace profiles with one-line competitor notes.
- Keep only the highest-impact evidence.
- Use "unknown" instead of lengthy caveats.

When the requested length is long:

- Add evidence register, richer profiles, pricing teardown, GTM analysis, and threat watchlist.
- Explain assumptions and confidence.
- Separate direct, adjacent, substitute, incumbent, and emerging competitors.
- Include validation questions and next research steps.

## Budget Allocation

For standard reports:

- 10-15%: assumptions and market definition.
- 25-35%: landscape matrix and competitor profiles.
- 25-30%: gaps, opportunities, and threats.
- 20-30%: recommendation, roadmap, and validation plan.

If the output is too long, preserve the recommendation and opportunity map before preserving competitor profile detail.

## User-Facing Length Note

If useful, open with one short note:

> 我按「标准简报」控制在约 2,000-3,000 字符，重点放在可执行定位和差异化机会。

Do not over-explain the length choice unless the user asked.
