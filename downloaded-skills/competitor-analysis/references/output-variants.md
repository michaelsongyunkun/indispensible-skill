# Output Variants

Use this reference to select the right output shape. Do not return every section by default; match the user's decision and time horizon.

## Quick Scan

Use for early idea checks or fast product judgment.

Target length: 800-1,400 Chinese characters or 500-900 English words. Usually one table and no long competitor profiles.

Output:

- Assumptions and market boundary.
- 3-5 closest competitors.
- One compact comparison table.
- 3 strongest threats.
- 3 differentiation opportunities.
- Recommendation: pursue, narrow, pivot, or pause.

## Strategic Brief

Default for product, pricing, and positioning decisions.

Target length: 1,800-3,500 Chinese characters or 1,200-2,200 English words. Include a matrix plus compact profiles.

Output:

- Executive take.
- Competitive landscape matrix.
- Profiles for 5-8 important competitors.
- Opportunity map.
- Positioning recommendation.
- 90-day moves and 12-18 month watchlist.

## Deep Diligence

Use for investment, launch, acquisition, or major roadmap decisions.

Target length: 4,500-9,000 Chinese characters or 3,000-6,000 English words. If more depth is needed, split into staged sections instead of returning an unreadable wall of text.

Output:

- Segmented market map.
- Direct, adjacent, substitute, incumbent, and emerging competitors.
- Evidence register with source quality.
- Scored matrix with rationale.
- Pricing and GTM teardown.
- Moat and momentum analysis.
- Risk register.
- Decision memo with go/no-go or strategic options.

## Sales Battlecard

Use when the output supports sales, fundraising, or stakeholder persuasion.

Target length: 1,200-2,500 Chinese characters or 800-1,600 English words. Favor sharp bullets over narrative.

Output:

- When we win.
- When we lose.
- Landmine questions to ask prospects.
- Competitor claims to expect.
- Objection handling.
- Proof points needed.
- "Choose us if..." and "choose them if..." framing.

## Pricing Teardown

Use when pricing, packaging, or business model matters.

Target length: 1,500-3,000 Chinese characters or 1,000-2,000 English words. Use tables for plan comparison and bullets for pricing implications.

Output:

- Pricing table by competitor.
- Packaging dimensions: free tier, trial, plan gates, usage limits, seats, add-ons.
- Buyer friction: unclear pricing, sales-only quote, hidden limits, upgrade triggers.
- Differentiation options: cheaper, premium, usage-based, outcome-based, bundle, freemium, concierge.
- Risk: race to bottom, margin pressure, enterprise procurement, support cost.

## Roadmap Input

Use when the user wants product strategy.

Target length: 1,500-3,500 Chinese characters or 1,000-2,200 English words. Prioritize decisions, sequencing, and tradeoffs over exhaustive competitor detail.

Output:

- Must-match features: table stakes.
- Avoid-copying features: expensive or low-value parity traps.
- Differentiating features: underserved jobs or workflows.
- Trust/proof features: credentials, guarantees, reporting, auditability.
- 30/60/90-day roadmap.
- Metrics to validate the wedge.

## Investor or Founder Memo

Use when deciding whether an idea is worth building.

Target length: 1,800-3,500 Chinese characters or 1,200-2,200 English words. Keep the recommendation crisp and evidence-backed.

Output:

- Why now.
- Why incumbents are weak.
- Why customers switch.
- Why distribution is plausible.
- What moat can compound.
- What could kill the idea.
- Crisp recommendation.
