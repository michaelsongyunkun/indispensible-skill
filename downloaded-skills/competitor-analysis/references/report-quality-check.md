# Report Quality Check

Use this reference before final delivery for strategic briefs, deep diligence, pricing teardowns, investor memos, or any high-stakes recommendation.

## Minimum Bar

The report should answer:

- What market/job is being analyzed?
- Who are the direct, adjacent, substitute, incumbent, and emerging competitors?
- What does each important competitor do better or worse?
- What evidence supports important claims?
- Where is the user's best wedge?
- What should the user build, avoid, price, position, or validate next?

## Final Checklist

| Check | Pass Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| Scope | customer, job, geography/platform/price band are stated or reasonably assumed |
| Competitive set | includes true substitutes, not only famous brands |
| Classification | competitors are labeled direct/adjacent/substitute/incumbent/emerging |
| Evidence | important claims have evidence labels, citations, or explicit unknowns |
| Current facts | pricing, funding, launches, reviews, and leadership are verified or marked unverified |
| Detail control | output length and depth match the user's request |
| Matrix quality | dimensions compare the same things across competitors |
| Scores | scores have a rubric or short rationale; no fake precision |
| Customer pain | opportunities map to actual underserved jobs or friction |
| Recommendation | names segment, wedge, proof needed, next moves, and risks |
| Honesty | uncertainty, stale data, and assumptions are visible |

## Quality Gates by Output Type

Quick scan:

- No more than one compact table.
- Recommendation appears near the top.
- Competitor notes are concise.

Strategic brief:

- Landscape matrix and opportunity map are both present.
- At least one specific wedge is recommended.
- 90-day moves and 12-18 month watchlist are included.

Deep diligence:

- Evidence register or source-quality notes are present.
- Unknowns and validation plan are explicit.
- Direct, adjacent, substitute, incumbent, and emerging competitors are considered.

Pricing teardown:

- Pricing plans, packaging gates, and buyer friction are separated.
- Risks such as margin pressure, support cost, and enterprise procurement are named.

Battlecard:

- Includes when we win, when we lose, trap questions, competitor claims, and objection handling.

## Red Flags

Revise before final delivery if the report:

- Lists competitors without explaining why they matter.
- Uses "market leader", "best", "dominant", or "fast-growing" without evidence.
- Gives a feature checklist but no strategic wedge.
- Treats "AI" as a differentiator without data, workflow, evaluation, or distribution proof.
- Ignores incumbents like spreadsheets, agencies, internal teams, consultants, friends, or doing nothing.
- Avoids a recommendation because the evidence is mixed.
- Invents market share, revenue, funding, user count, pricing, or traction.

## Repair Moves

- Missing evidence: add labels and source notes, or mark unknown.
- Too long: preserve recommendation, matrix, opportunity map; compress profiles.
- Too shallow: add customer pain, pricing/GTM, trust, and distribution analysis.
- Wrong competitor set: re-scope by customer and job-to-be-done.
- Weak conclusion: choose a primary wedge and name the validation needed.
