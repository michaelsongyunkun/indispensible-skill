# Research Query Patterns

Use this reference before web research or when the competitive set, pricing, reviews, traction, funding, docs, or customer sentiment must be discovered.

## Research Sequence

1. Define the job-to-be-done and buyer.
2. Search broad category terms to find obvious direct competitors.
3. Search customer pain and workflow terms to find substitutes.
4. Search pricing, reviews, docs, changelog, integrations, funding, and jobs for each major competitor.
5. Cross-check with source quality labels.
6. Stop when additional sources repeat the same landscape or when the decision is supported.

## Query Patterns

Replace bracketed terms with the market, product, customer, or competitor name.

| Goal | Query Patterns |
| --- | --- |
| Direct competitors | `[category] software`, `[job to be done] tool`, `best [category] tools`, `[product] alternatives`, `[product] competitors` |
| Substitutes | `how do [customer] solve [job]`, `[job] spreadsheet template`, `[job] agency`, `[job] consultant`, `[job] manual workflow` |
| Pricing | `[competitor] pricing`, `[competitor] plans`, `[competitor] free tier`, `[competitor] enterprise pricing` |
| Product depth | `[competitor] docs`, `[competitor] changelog`, `[competitor] integrations`, `[competitor] API`, `[competitor] demo` |
| Customer pain | `[competitor] reviews`, `[competitor] complaints`, `[competitor] limitations`, `[competitor] reddit`, `[competitor] G2 reviews` |
| Momentum | `[competitor] funding`, `[competitor] acquisition`, `[competitor] jobs`, `[competitor] release notes`, `[competitor] launch` |
| Trust/compliance | `[competitor] security`, `[competitor] SOC 2`, `[competitor] privacy`, `[competitor] terms`, `[competitor] case study` |
| Distribution | `[competitor] partners`, `[competitor] affiliate`, `[competitor] marketplace`, `[competitor] app store`, `[competitor] templates` |

## Source Mix

For each important competitor, try to collect:

- Official positioning and pricing.
- Product depth from docs, demos, changelog, app pages.
- Customer voice from reviews, forums, app stores, or social posts.
- Momentum from news, hiring, launches, funding, or content cadence.
- Trust signals from security, privacy, case studies, customer logos, credentials.

## Query Expansion

When results are thin:

- Search adjacent terms and old category names.
- Search by customer role: `for counselors`, `for sales teams`, `for parents`, `for creators`.
- Search by workflow verb: plan, generate, audit, compare, match, monitor, export, automate.
- Search by price pain: affordable, free, open source, enterprise, agency alternative.
- Search by failure mode: inaccurate, slow, hard to use, not integrated, expensive.

## Stop Conditions

Stop broad research when:

- The direct competitor set is stable across multiple sources.
- Pricing and feature claims are confirmed or marked unknown.
- Customer pain themes repeat.
- The recommendation would not change with another source.

For high-stakes work, keep a source register and state remaining unknowns.

## Compliance Guardrails

- Use public or authorized sources.
- Respect robots, terms, and rate limits.
- Do not use hidden tokens, leaked documents, private accounts, or unauthorized scraping.
- If a source is paywalled or inaccessible, mark it unavailable rather than working around access controls.
