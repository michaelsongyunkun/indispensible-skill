# Arc Fatigue Control

Use this reference when several fatigue symptoms appear together: continuous pressure, high information density, repeated chapter structure, and private relationships that feel too tight or tactical.

## Reader Fatigue Audit

Reader fatigue usually comes from several tracks being maxed at once. Diagnose before rewriting:

| Track | Red flag | Fix direction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pressure curve | 3+ chapters in a row are public, dangerous, accusatory, deadline-driven, or externally squeezed. | Insert aftermath, routine, private repair, strategy, training, travel, body recovery, or quiet dread. |
| Information load | Each chapter foregrounds multiple clues, symbols, institutions, rules, names, and reversals. | Reduce to 1-2 foreground facts; move other material to texture or later payoff. |
| Pattern structure | Chapters solve through the same setup -> recognition -> proof -> counterpressure -> hook shape. | Change protagonist mode, solution mode, antagonist mode, or hook mode. |
| Private relationships | Characters only talk under crisis, accusation, clue exchange, or tactical bargaining. | Add low-stakes texture, care, banter, shared labor, small embarrassment, changed address, or silence. |

## Sequence Load Map

For any 5-10 chapter run, map:

| Ch | Pressure level | Info load | Pattern mode | Relationship texture | Relief value |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Low / medium / high | Low / medium / high | Investigation / confrontation / aftermath / relationship / strategy / training / resource / silence | Tactical / private / loose / intimate / strained / absent | What lets the reader breathe? |

If pressure and information load are both high for three chapters, plan relief. If relationship texture is "tactical" for most rows, loosen private scenes. If pattern mode repeats, swap one chapter engine.

## Relief Chapter Design

A relief chapter is not filler. It lowers external pressure while changing one durable thing:

- Relationship: trust, debt, intimacy, address, jealousy, shared secret, apology, care, or distance.
- Body: injury care, failed sleep, hunger, training soreness, medicine side effects, cultivation backlash.
- Strategy: revised plan, abandoned plan, new rule, moral line, private vow.
- Resource: repaired object, spent money, hidden token, borrowed room, missing pill, damaged ledger.
- World texture: route, market, meal, craft, weather, sect routine, servant gossip, quiet ritual.

Keep one small hook, but do not stack a new major threat, new major clue, and new major symbol in the same relief chapter.

## Private Relationship Looseness

Looseness means characters can exist together without every line serving evidence or crisis. Add:

- A practical task done together.
- A small disagreement that is not plot-critical.
- A joke, habit, silence, or bodily detail that reveals comfort or discomfort.
- A changed form of address.
- Care that one character accepts badly.
- A moment where someone almost says the important thing and chooses the smaller thing.

Private looseness should still move the story through trust, shame, debt, affection, suspicion, or resolve.

## Integrated Fix Order

When all four symptoms appear, fix in this order:

1. Map the sequence load.
2. Break any repeated chapter engine.
3. Choose one relief chapter or relief scene.
4. Reduce the information foreground for the densest chapter.
5. Add or revise one private relationship beat so it changes trust, debt, distance, or address.

Do not solve fatigue only with line edits. The issue is usually structural.
