# Chapter Sequence Audit

Use this reference when auditing 3+ chapters or a long arc for repetitive structure, unclear movement, pacing fatigue, hook sameness, weak relationship progression, or continuity drift.

## Audit Table

Start with a compact audit table:

| Ch | Function | Pressure level | Info load | Relationship texture | Opening pressure | Protagonist mode | Solution mode | Antagonist mode | Hook mode | Real change |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Investigation / aftermath / strategy / relationship / training / confrontation | Low / medium / high | Low / medium / high | Tactical / private / loose / intimate / strained / absent | What starts pressure? | Detects, misreads, bargains, fails, protects, retreats, acts first | Evidence, cost, trust, resource, public act, moral choice | Escalates, withdraws, feigns kindness, goes silent, changes arena | Threat, consequence, relationship, dread, choice, body cost | Plot, relationship, status, resource, knowledge, emotion |

If the "Real change" column is thin, the chapter may be busy but not moving the story. If several rows repeat the same modes, the sequence will feel mechanical.

## Movement Checks

For each chapter, identify at least one durable movement:

- Plot: a plan changes, an obstacle changes, a question narrows, a deadline appears.
- Relationship: trust, debt, intimacy, address, alliance, shame, jealousy, or distance changes.
- Status: reputation, rank, protection, public knowledge, or institutional position changes.
- Resource: money, medicine, artifact, body condition, leverage, time, or sanctuary changes.
- Knowledge: someone learns, misreads, withholds, or reinterprets something.
- Emotion: a feeling shifts into action, avoidance, resolve, doubt, or confession.

Chapters that only repeat pressure without durable movement usually need a new function.

## Repetition Diagnosis

Mark repeated patterns:

| Symptom | Likely fix |
| --- | --- |
| Same trap-recognition-proof loop | Replace one chapter with aftermath, misread, relationship, or strategy. |
| Same antagonist escalation | Use silence, kindness, legalism, proxy pressure, retreat, or public favor. |
| Same protagonist competence beat | Let the protagonist fail, ask for help, act first, misjudge cost, or win the wrong thing. |
| Same hook type | Rotate to consequence, body cost, relationship turn, quiet dread, or choice. |
| Same clue delivery | Use mistaken witness, reluctant ally, object cost, public ritual, or withheld evidence. |
| Same emotional note | Track emotional progression: trigger -> suppression -> choice -> leak -> aftereffect. |
| Pressure and info both stay high | Insert relief, split dense clues, or move one chapter into relationship, aftermath, strategy, or quiet dread. |
| Private relationships stay tactical | Add looseness: care, shared labor, small humor, changed address, low-stakes disagreement, or silence. |

## Priority Fixes

After the audit table, recommend only the highest-leverage fixes:

1. Fix broken or blurry main-line causality.
2. Break repeated chapter engines across the sequence.
3. Add durable relationship/status/resource movement.
4. Reduce overloaded clue/symbol stacks and lower continuous pressure.
5. Loosen private relationships, then smooth transitions and vary emotional rendering.

Avoid giving ten equally weighted suggestions. A useful audit tells the writer what to change first.

## Replacement Chapter Engines

When the sequence is too samey, swap in one of these:

- **Aftermath chapter**: consequence settles; cost becomes visible.
- **Relationship chapter**: trust, debt, affection, shame, or distance changes.
- **Strategy chapter**: the protagonist revises the plan without immediate confrontation.
- **Misread chapter**: the protagonist gets one key meaning wrong.
- **Failure chapter**: the protagonist wins information but loses time, trust, body condition, or moral standing.
- **Enemy silence chapter**: expected escalation does not arrive, creating dread.
- **Resource chapter**: medicine, money, shelter, token, body, or leverage becomes scarce.
- **Training/craft chapter**: skill, body, technique, or profession creates a new limit.
- **Public ritual chapter**: rules, etiquette, rank, and witnesses change the stakes.
- **Quiet dread chapter**: nothing explodes, but one calm detail alters the reader's expectation.

## Output Shape

For audit requests, prefer:

1. One-paragraph diagnosis.
2. Audit table.
3. Repetition risks.
4. Priority fixes.
5. Optional replacement outline for 2-4 chapters.

For rewrite requests, first identify which chapters need new engines, then rewrite only the requested scope.
