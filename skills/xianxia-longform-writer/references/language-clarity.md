# Language Clarity

Use this reference when prose feels obscure, hard to understand, over-abstract, overly ornate, or concept-heavy, while the user still wants strong literary quality rather than watery prose.

## Clarity Without Flattening

Clear prose is not the same as plain prose. Aim for:

| Keep clear | Keep rich |
| --- | --- |
| Who is acting. | Precise image. |
| What changed. | Rhythm and sentence variety. |
| Why the moment matters. | Subtext and implication. |
| What the character chooses or avoids. | Character-specific perception and voice. |
| How one beat causes the next. | Atmosphere when it changes action or emotion. |

Do not remove all metaphor, ambiguity, or lyricism. Remove fog around action, cause, stakes, and emotional turns.

## Story Legibility

Before polishing language, make the scene readable as story:

- Name the acting subject when a sentence becomes ambiguous.
- Put the decisive action close to the character who performs it.
- Make cause and consequence visible.
- Tie abstract feelings or ideas to a choice, gesture, object, or sensory detail.
- Let mystery come from what is withheld, not from unclear grammar.

If a paragraph sounds beautiful but the reader cannot tell what happened, revise it.

## Sentence Opacity Audit

Watch for these opacity sources:

| Symptom | Revision move |
| --- | --- |
| Too many abstractions in one sentence | Replace one abstraction with action, object, or bodily perception. |
| Metaphor hides the literal event | State the event plainly, then keep one sharp image. |
| Long sentence buries the turn | Split at the choice, reveal, or reversal. |
| Pronouns or subjects blur | Name the actor, object, or target. |
| Mood replaces movement | Add a decision, interruption, gesture, or consequence. |
| Explanation repeats what action shows | Cut the explanation or turn it into a changed behavior. |

## High-Quality Plainness

Avoid watery prose by keeping plain sentences charged:

- Use concrete verbs rather than generic explanation.
- Let word choice fit status, relationship, and setting.
- Keep one memorable image per important beat.
- Preserve subtext in dialogue; do not make characters explain everything.
- Use short sentences for pressure or recognition, not for bland summary.
- Keep specificity: object, gesture, social rule, place, body, weather, texture.

Bad simplification removes texture. Good simplification reveals the story's pressure.

## Revision Moves

When a passage is hard to understand:

1. Identify the literal event in one sentence.
2. Identify the emotional or tactical turn.
3. Rewrite the paragraph so the event and turn are visible.
4. Restore one precise image, rhythm, or subtextual line.
5. Cut any sentence that only restates mood without changing perception, choice, or consequence.

## Quick Example

Before:

```text
那盏灯在他心里延伸成一口井，井底浮着旧日的账，影子替每个人签下无法偿还的名字。
```

Clearer but still textured:

```text
林烬看着那盏灯，忽然想起账册最后一页的空格。那不是遗漏，是等人来认。井口的冷气贴着他的手背，他终于明白，签名的人未必欠债，也可能是祭品。
```

The revision keeps lamp, ledger, well, and dread, but gives the reader action, perception, inference, and changed understanding.
